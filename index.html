Toronto

Geography/History

Toronto is the capital city of the Canadian province of Ontario, located in the central part of the province on the northwestern shore of Lake Ontario. The city covers an area of 630 square kilometers and has a population of over 2.9 million people, making it the largest city in Canada and the fourth-largest in North America.

area now known as Toronto has been inhabited by Indigenous peoples for thousands of years. The Anishinaabe people were the first to settle in the area, followed by the Haudenosaunee and the Wendat. The French were the first Europeans to explore the area in the early 1600s, followed by the British in the mid-1700s.

In 1793, the British established the town of York on the site of present-day Toronto. The town grew quickly and was renamed Toronto in 1834. Toronto became the capital of the province of Ontario in 1867, following Canadian Confederation.

Throughout the 20th century, Toronto grew rapidly and became a major economic and cultural center in Canada. The city has hosted several international events, including the 2015 Pan American Games and the 2017 Invictus Games. Today, Toronto is known for its diverse population, vibrant arts and culture scene, and thriving business community.

Toronto is situated on the northern shore of Lake Ontario, one of the five Great Lakes, and is connected to the St. Lawrence River system. The city is located in a region known as the Golden Horseshoe, which is home to over 8.7 million people and encompasses several major cities and urban centers in southern Ontario.
